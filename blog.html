Types of food license

In India, food businesses must obtain a Food License from the Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI). There are three types of food licenses based on the size and nature of the business:

1. FSSAI Basic Registration

For: Small food businesses (annual turnover ≤ ₹12 lakh).

Applicable to:

Small manufacturers, retailers, distributors, and home-based food sellers.

Small food stalls, petty vendors, and cottage industries.

Issued by: State Government.

2. FSSAI State License

For: Medium-sized businesses (annual turnover ₹12 lakh – ₹20 crore).

Applicable to:

Medium-scale food manufacturers, storage units, transporters, retailers, restaurants, and caterers.

Food processing units (capacity limits apply).

Issued by: State Government.

3. FSSAI Central License

For: Large businesses (annual turnover > ₹20 crore).

Applicable to:

Large-scale manufacturers, importers, exporters, food chains, and big restaurants.

Businesses operating in multiple states.

Government institutions like railways, airports, and seaports.

Issued by: Central FSSAI Authority.
